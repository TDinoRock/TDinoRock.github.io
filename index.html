My Portfolio

My name is Trennon Talbot, and I am a Sophomore student at Boise State University. My major is in Games, Interactive Media, and Mobile Technology's, or GIMM for short. Now that introductions are out of the way, this is my portfolio website showcasing my various programming projects from 2D platformer to VR open world. My most recent work has been developing "Accurate Weaponry."

Accurate Weaponry

Accurate Weaponry is a physics-based, open-world VR game that redefines medieval combat with a focus on realistic technique, strength, and endurance. I learn a lot about VR technology and tracking as well as the difficulty to replicate real-world physics we interact with every day. I also learned how to program AI to be responsive to specific situations and control their difficulty. I had a lot of fun researching every weapon's techniques and creating training exercises to help the player how to use it.

Made in Unity in C# language and with Meta Quest 2 & Quest 3

Marine Training Simulator

Marine Training Simulator is a very basic 2-player local First Person Shooter. I learned how to make several different features like riquocheting lasers of shields, creating corresponding UI elements and indicators for each player. These indicators are for when the player can dash and when the player can shoot. I also learned a lot about raycasting. There were many frustrations and bugs that I had to work through in such a short time frame, but I had lots of fun seeing how much fun my classmates had playing it.

Made in Unity in C# language

Brain Rot

Brain Rot is a short VR game where you are sitting a class room listening to a lecture. During the lecture, brain rot videos start popping up around the player creating visual and audio distractions. The player must delete these brain rots and score 100% on the quiz at the end of the lecture. This project started my drive for VR and what I can create on that platform. So I learned the basics of VR, picking objects up, and snapping objects to certain locations. I had lots of fun playing playing it and eliminating the brain rots.

Made in Unity in C# language and with Meta Quest 2
